I. Core Resource Sectors (Canadian Context)

1. Fisheries & Mariculture

Focuses on the transition from traditional wild-capture fisheries to sustainable aquaculture. Key management issues include quota allocation, bycatch mitigation, and habitat protection in Atlantic Canadian waters.

2. Forestry & Wildlife Management

Examination of sustainable harvest levels (AAC) and the protection of biodiversity. Wildlife management focuses on habitat fragmentation and the role of "protected areas" in maintaining species persistence.

3. Water & Agriculture Policy

Analysis of agricultural runoff effects on local watersheds. Emphasis is placed on nitrogen/phosphorus management and its downstream impact on freshwater habitats.

II. Deep Dive: Atlantic Salmon (The Keystone Case Study)

1. Ecological Significance as a Bioindicator

Atlantic salmon (Salmo salar) are recognized as a "Ridge-to-Reef" biological metric. Monitoring follows three primary vectors:

  • Physical Parameters: Water temperature (critical limit < 23°C), flow velocity, and river connectivity.
  • Ecological Indicators: Food availability (capelin/sandlance), reproductive success, and smolt migration health.
  • Ecotoxicology: Tissue analysis for pathogens (Sea Lice, ISAV) and contaminants (PCBs, heavy metals).

2. Management Strategies

  • Habitat Restoration: Removal of aging dams and restoration of natural stream morphology.
  • Genetic Protection: Implementing barriers and monitoring to prevent farm-escapees from diluting wild genetic pools.

III. Sustainable Export Standards & Quality Control

1. HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point)

A preventative logic system mandatory for global export (US, EU, Japan). Key CCPs for salmon include:

Control Stage Metric Rationale
Initial Chilling < 4°C Prevents bacterial proliferation and histamines.
Parasite Control -20°C (24h) Required for Sashimi-grade (Japan/US) status.
Traceability SIMP Compliance Ensures the product is not from IUU (Illegal) fishing.

2. International Certifications

  • ISO 9001/22000: Global standards for Quality and Food Safety Management systems.
  • ASC (Aquaculture Stewardship Council): Certifies that the mariculture process has minimal social and environmental impact.
