# DISPUTE OF ARBITRARY ASSESSMENT: PAYROLL ACCOUNT RP0001

**To:** Canada Revenue Agency - Collections / Garnishment Division
**Regarding Business Number:** 785581745RP0001
**Date:** April 20, 2026

---

## 1. STATEMENT OF DISPUTE

This document serves as a formal dispute of the arbitrary assessment totaling **$40,047.51** issued on or about April 15, 2026. This assessment is factually incorrect and based on an account that was confirmed closed prior to the assessment period.

## 2. EVIDENCE OF ERROR

As of April 14, 2026, the Canada Revenue Agency confirmed through official documentation that the payroll account associated with **Business Number 785581745RP0001** has been successfully closed.

**Verified Key Figures:**

* **Request/Confirmation Date:** April 14, 2026
* **Effective Closing Date:** **October 31, 2023**
* **Confirmation Type:** Official CRA Confirmation of Closure

## 3. LEGAL ARGUMENT

The Requirement to Pay (RTP) and the subsequent arbitrary assessment were issued against a business entity that had no active payroll obligations, no employees, and an officially closed "RP" account.

According to the CRA's own records (Exhibit A: `Request to close payroll account – confirmation.pdf`), no payroll remittances were required for the period of 2024 or 2025 as the account ceased to exist as an active entity on October 31, 2023.

## 4. REQUESTED ACTION

1. **Immediate Nullification:** We request the immediate nullification of the $40,047.51 arbitrary payroll assessment.
2. **Release of Requirement to Pay (RTP):** Upon the correction of this mathematical error, the remaining GST/HST liability (~$28,000) will be addressed via a formal payment plan. We request the immediate issuance of a **Release Letter** for the CIBC bank freeze.
3. **Collection Officer Peer Review:** We request that this file be reviewed by the assigned collections officer to acknowledge the administrative error in assessing a closed account.
