# PROVINCIAL LEGAL REBUTTAL: WAGE CLAIM DEFENSE

**To:** Department of Post-Secondary Education, Training and Labour (PETL), New Brunswick
**Subject:** Rebuttal of Wage Claims – Case Refs C2024-00491 & C2024-00859
**Notice Date:** April 20, 2026

## 📜 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document serves as a formal rebuttal to the wage claims filed by **Byron Panagapka** and **Jacquelin Estimable**. The claimants were engaged as independent **B2B Piecework Subcontractors**, not employees. As such, they are not eligible for wage protections under the Employment Standards Act.

## ⚖️ 2. CONTRACTUAL FOUNDATION

Both claimants executed legally binding **Piecework Agreements**. Under these contracts:

- **Tools & Logistics:** The subcontractors provided their own vehicles, fuel, and specialized equipment.
- **Independence:** The subcontractors had the right to accept or refuse specific "Pieces" of work without penalty.
- **Liability:** The subcontractors were responsible for their own workmanship and remedial actions.

## 📈 3. FORENSIC AUDIT (PAYMENT VERIFICATION)

As documented in the **RBC Forensic Matrix (2025)**, the payments to these individuals were categorized as "Subcontractor Outflows" (COGS), not payroll.

## 🚨 4. REQUESTED ACTIONS

1. **Dismissal of Claims:** Categorize the claimants as Independent Contractors based on the signed agreements.
2. **Cessation of Demand:** Rescind the "Order to Pay" pending a secondary review of the contractor status receipts.

**Document Finalized.**
