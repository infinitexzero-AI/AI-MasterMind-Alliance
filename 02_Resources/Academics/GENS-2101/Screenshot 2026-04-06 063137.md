# Tree Growth and Carbon
- Trees remove carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the atmosphere through photosynthesis, incorporating it into their stems, branches, leaves, and roots.
- Wood is approximately 50% carbon by weight.
- Within a single tree, typically 50-65% of total carbon is found in the stem, and 20-30% is in the woody roots.
- The total amount of carbon in a stand of trees is highly dependent on age; young trees contain little carbon, but a dense stand of large trees can store up to 1,100 tonnes carbon dioxide equivalent per hectare (tCO2e ha⁻¹).
