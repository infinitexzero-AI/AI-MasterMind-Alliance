# Great Lakes - St. Lawrence Basin Map
- The map illustrates the Great Lakes and St. Lawrence River system, encompassing major lakes (Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, Ontario) and their connecting rivers (St. Mary's, St. Clair, Detroit, Niagara, Ottawa, St. Lawrence, St. Maurice).
- It delineates various sub-basins, including those for each of the Great Lakes and key rivers, within the larger Great Lakes-St. Lawrence Drainage Area.
- The geographic scope covers parts of U.S. states (e.g., Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, New York, Ohio, Pennsylvania) and Canadian provinces (Ontario, Quebec), with international and provincial/state borders clearly marked.
- Significant infrastructure is highlighted, such as Compensating Works near Sault Ste. Marie, the Moses-Saunders Dam on the St. Lawrence River, and International Niagara Control Works.
- Numerous cities are identified, including Duluth, Chicago, Detroit, Toronto, Ottawa, Montreal, and Quebec City (implied near Trois-Rivières), providing geographical context. A legend explains map symbols for structures, cities, channels, borders, lakes, and rivers, complemented by a scale bar.
