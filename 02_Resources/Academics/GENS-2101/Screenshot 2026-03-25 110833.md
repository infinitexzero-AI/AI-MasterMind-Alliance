# Agricultural Production: China and India
- **China:** Utilizes less than 10% of the world's arable land to produce 25% of the world's grain. In 2020, China was the leading producer for over 30 crops, including key staples like wheat, rice, tomatoes, and potatoes.
- **India:** In 2016, the agriculture sector constituted 23% of the country's GDP and employed 59% of its workforce, with approximately 70% of the rural population relying on agriculture. In 2020, India produced half of the world's mangoes (24.7 million tons), 20% of global wheat (107.5 million tons), and a quarter of the global output for rice (178.3 million tons), sugar cane (370.5 million tons), and bananas (31.5 million tons).
