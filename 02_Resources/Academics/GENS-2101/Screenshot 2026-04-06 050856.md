# Terrestrial Storage of Carbon

Terrestrial ecosystems play a crucial role in the global carbon cycle by storing carbon. Researchers mapped this distribution using satellite data and estimates of carbon in vegetation and soil.

Key points about terrestrial carbon storage:
- Highest concentrations are found in tropical and boreal regions.
- In the tropics, carbon is predominantly stored in vegetation.
- In boreal regions, especially peatlands, most carbon is stored in soils.
- Forested ecosystems generally store more carbon than unforested areas.

The accompanying map, titled 'Global Terrestrial Storage of Carbon', visually represents carbon storage density (kg/m²) worldwide, with a color legend indicating ranges from 0.1-10 (light yellow) to >40 (dark red/brown), including a 'No data' category.
