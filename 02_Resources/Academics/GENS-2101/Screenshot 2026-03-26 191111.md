# Biophysical

This slide presents a framework for analyzing biophysical factors, dividing them into stable/predictable and unstable/unpredictable categories, each with corresponding questions for investigation.

## Stable/predictable factors:
- **Rainfall regime:** Questions include the amount, timing, location, and intensity of rainfall.
- **Soil types:** Questions focus on characteristics such as thinness, thickness, porosity, or clay content.
- **Geomorphology:** The primary question is about where groundwater is recharging.
- **Evaporative demand:** Inquiries cover whether it is high or low at different times of the year.
- **Pests of various kinds (bugs, weeds, fungus, etc):** Questions ask if these are manageable and predictable, and if they are seasonal or year-round.

## Unstable/less or unpredictable factors:
- **Drought:** The question is whether records reveal patterns.
- **El Nino/La Nina:** Inquiries focus on how the area is impacted and what written records or oral history reveal.
- **Flood:** The question is whether records reveal patterns.
- **Pests (accidentally introduced; emergent due to 'tipping point'):** Questions ask if these are manageable and with whose help.
