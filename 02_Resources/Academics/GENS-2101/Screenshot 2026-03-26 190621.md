# Table 3.1 Water Productivity and Crop Water Requirements
- This table details water productivity (m³ of green water flow per tonne grain) and crop water requirements (mm) for major food crops worldwide.
- Crops are classified by their hydroclimate type (Temperate or Tropical) and photosynthetic pathway (C3 or C4).
- Key information provided for each crop includes:
    - **Water productivity:** A range and average value in m³ per tonne grain (e.g., Wheat: 780-2640 m³/tonne grain, average 1480).
    - **Yield range:** In tonnes per hectare (t/ha) (e.g., Wheat: 4-6 t/ha, Maize: 6-9 t/ha, Sugarcane: 50-150 t/ha).
    - **Water requirements:** In millimeters (mm) (e.g., Wheat: 450-650 mm, Rice: 500-950 mm, Oranges: 600-950 mm).
- The table covers a diverse set of crops, broadly categorized as:
    - **Temperate:** Wheat, Barley, Rye, Oats, Rapeseed, Temperate cereals, Green Beans, Green Peas, Potatoes.
    - **Tropical:** Rice, Maize, Millet, Sorghum, Tropical cereals, Sugarcane, Cotton seed, Sunflower seed, Dry Beans, Soya beans, Banana/Plantain, Bananas, Oranges.
- Data sources are cited for various entries, including Rockström et al., Gleick, Doorenbos and Kassam, and Pimentel et al.
- A general note references Doorenbos and Kassam (1986) and Doorenbos and Pruitt (1992).
