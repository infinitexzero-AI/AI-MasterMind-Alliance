# Understanding Your Interests: Personal Project Pitch

**Name**: [User Name]

**Proposed Topic**: Comparative Urban Water Governance: Responding to Scarcity and Risk in the Anthropocene  

## 1. Description of Interests

My primary interest in Natural Resources Management lies at the intersection of **urbanization** and **water security**. Specifically, I am interested in how cities can transform their "command and control" infrastructure into adaptive, socio-ecological systems that can withstand the uncertainties of climate change. I am particularly drawn to the tension between economic development (the "hydraulic mission") and the preservation of ecosystem services (such as natural flood mitigation provided by wetlands).

## 2. Proposed Challenge/Issue

I propose to study the challenge of **Urban Water Resilience** by comparing two distinct geographical and socio-economic contexts:

1. **Local Context (Sackville, NB / Tantramar Region)**: Focusing on coastal resilience, wetland management, and the historical "dykeland" infrastructure. how local governance balances agricultural needs with flood protection in a rising sea-level scenario.
2. **Global Context (Cape Town, South Africa)**: Analyzing the "Day Zero" water crisis as a case study in managing extreme scarcity under political and social inequality.

## 3. Socio-Ecological Dimensions

This project will explore:

- **Ecological**: The role of wetlands and aquifers as natural buffers vs. engineered solutions.
- **Social/Political**: The "constellation of social forces" (states, private sector, civil society) that dictate water allocation during crises.
- **Knowledge Systems**: How local/indigenous knowledge (e.g., Mi’kmaq perspectives on the Tantramar) can be integrated with hydrologic modeling.

## 4. Potential Sources

## 5. Goals

My goal is to provide a set of "best practice" recommendations for adaptive urban water governance that could be applied in both developed and developing world contexts.
