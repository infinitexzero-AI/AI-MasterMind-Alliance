# India Case Study: CBFM
- A hybrid system where community forestry exists alongside centralized forest governance.
- The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 (often called the Forest Rights Act or FRA), came into force in 2008.
- The act recognizes and vests forest rights—including the occupation of forest land—in tribes and communities that have been residing in, cultivating, and conserving the forests for generations.
- Claims can be made by Scheduled Tribes that primarily reside in and depend on forests for their livelihood, and any individual or community residing on forest land for at least three generations (75 years) prior to December 13, 2005.
- The act recognizes three types of forest rights: individual forest rights (IFR), community forest rights (CFR), and community forest resource rights (CFRR).
