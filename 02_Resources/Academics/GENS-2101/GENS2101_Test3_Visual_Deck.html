💧 Module 1: Blue vs. Green Water

  • Blue Water: Surface and groundwater (rivers, aquifers).
  • Green Water: Soil moisture captured by plants (60% of global rainfall).
  • The foundation of global food security is Green Water.

💧 Water's 6 Fundamental Characteristics

According to Savenije, water is:

  • Essential (No substitutes)
  • Fugitive (Always moving/evaporating)
  • Indivisible (Holistic system)
  • Bulky (High transport cost)
  • Non-substitutable
  • Complex (Legal & social layers)

⚖️ The Dublin Principles

  • 1. Finite & Vulnerable: Essential to life and development.
  • 2. Participatory: Involving users, planners, and policy-makers.
  • 3. Role of Women: Central to provision, management, and safeguarding.
  • 4. Economic & Social Good: Value in use, but also a social right.

⚖️ The RIDe Framework

The feedback loop of water management:

  • Resources: The quantity and quality of the water base.
  • Infrastructure: Hardware/software to treat and convey water.
  • Demand: Requirements of users and their institutions.

⚖️ The Right to Water

Elements of a proposed right (Scanlon et al. 2003):

  • Accessibility: Safe reach, affordable, and legal.
  • Adequate Quality: Safe for personal/domestic use.
  • Adequate Quantity: Sufficient and continuous supply.

📊 Strategic Water Audits

  • Identify current status and trends in demand/use.
  • Identify Externalities (macro temporal/spatial).
  • Debunk Hydrological Myths or misconceptions.
  • Assess efficacy of policies and drought strategies.

⚖️ Benefits of Participation

Beyond the "feel-good" factor (Principle 2):

  • Technical success (scale, design, maintenance).
  • Cost Recovery: Essential for revenue and financing.
  • Rights protection (Human, Environmental, Cultural).
  • Transparency and Accountability in decision-making.

⚖️ The "Consensus Trap"

  • Participation != Consensus: Conflict resolution is still required.
  • The Capacity Gap: marginalized groups need an "Enabling Environment."
  • Marginalized groups: Poor, Indigenous, Women, Elderly.
  • Critical factors: Time, Place, and Pace of change.

🌊 Module 1.6: Wetlands

Areas of marsh, fen, peatland, or water:

  • The 6m Rule: Marine depth < 6m at low tide.
  • Ramsar Convention: The intergovernmental treaty for protection.
  • Includes: Coral Reefs, Seagrass, Kelp Forests, Rivers, and Mires.

🌊 Global State of Wetlands

  • 22% Loss (411 million ha) since 1970.
  • 0.52% Annual Decline.
  • 25% of remaining wetlands are in "Poor condition" internationally.
  • Restoration is complex due to hydrological disruption.

🇨🇦 Ontario Snapshot: Golden Horseshoe

  • 85% Loss in SW Ontario settled regions.
  • Golden Horseshoe: Homes 54% of Ontario's population.
  • Acceleration: 7,300+ ha lost between 2011 and 2015.

💰 Module 1.7: Wetland Economics

Valuation of natural services (OMNRF, 2017):

  • Wetlands: 40.6% of total global ecosystem value.
  • The Efficiency Gap: Ontario filtration ($4.2B) vs Building Replacements ($2.9B).
  • Services: Flood mitigation, carbon sinks, fisheries.

🐸 The Hierarchy (Sequence)

Must be applied in this strict order:

  • 1. AVOID: No impact to existing wetlands.
  • 2. Minimize: Reduce unavoidable scale of impact.
  • 3. Mitigate: Repair/remedy damage.
  • 4. OFFSET: The residual **last resort**.

🏦 Offsetting Comparison

  • Proponent-Led: Developer is responsible; varying success.
  • In-Lieu Fee: Pay an NGO/Gov; concern with **Time Lag**.
  • Mitigation Banking: Buy credits; highest feasibility (pre-existing).

🌲 Module 2: Sustainable Forestry

Management balancing the Triad Goal:

  • Ecological Integrity: Biodiversity & ecosystems.
  • Economic Stability: Timber supply & jobs.
  • Social Values: Indigenous rights & recreation.

🌲 The 94% Ownership Rule

  • 90% Provincial Control: The primary management authority.
  • 2% Federal (Parks/Military).
  • 2% Territorial.
  • Only 6% is private property.

🪓 The Allowable Annual Cut

The regulatory harvest limit set every 5-10 years:

  • Based on Timber Supply Analysis.
  • Set by Chief Foresters of the Provinces.
  • Must subtract land for "non-timber" values (habitats).

✂️ Clearcutting vs. Selection

  • Clearcutting: Efficient; mimics natural fire; visual impact; stream warming.
  • Selection Cutting: Continuous canopy; protects biodiversity; high cost; high skill required.

🏔️ Harrop-Procter Community Forest

  • Priority: Domestic Watershed Health.
  • Mechanism: Shifted power to the community.
  • Technique: Selective logging & Ecosystem-Based Management.

🇮🇳 The FRA 75-Year Rule

Crucial for "Non-Tribal" claimants:

  • Must prove residence/dependence for **75 years** (3 generations).
  • Cutoff Date: **December 13, 2005**.
  • Requires mandatory consent from the **Gram Sabha** for any land diversion.

🛰️ Border Exemptions (FCAA 2023)

  • Exempts forest land within **100km** of international borders.
  • Specifically impacts **Sikkim (47%)** and **Uttarakhand (45%)** forest cover.
  • Allows infrastructure development without standard environmental clearances.

🏗️ Urban Metabolism & Entropy

The Wolman/Georgescu-Roegen Synthesis:

  • Crystalline Inflows: High-quality, low-entropy Water & Energy.
  • Chaotic Outflows: High-entropy Waste, Heat, Sewage.
  • Cities maintain internal order by dumping disorder into the surrounding environment.

⛏️ The Mineral Paradox

  • Paradox: Green energy transition requires a massive increase in mineral mining.
  • Resource Curse: Countries with high mineral wealth often face poor institutions and slower growth.

Indigenous Forest
  • Diffuse, multi-directional energy.

Oil Palm Plantation

  • Entropy exported (disorderly).
