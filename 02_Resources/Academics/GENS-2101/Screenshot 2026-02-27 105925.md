# St. Croix River Basin Map
- Detailed geographical map illustrating the St. Croix River Basin, which forms part of the international border between Maine (USA) and New Brunswick (Canada).
- The map outlines the St. Croix River's course from its headwaters (including North Lake, Grand Lake, and Spednic Lake) to Passamaquoddy Bay and the Bay of Fundy.
- Key hydrological features include various lakes (e.g., Sysladobsis Lake, West Grand Lake, Big Lake) and prominent streams (e.g., Tomah Stream, Grand Lake Stream).
- Major cities and towns such as Danforth, Vanceboro, McAdam, Princeton, Saint Stephen, Calais, and Saint Andrews are marked.
- Significant man-made structures along the river, specifically dams (Forest City Dam, Vanceboro Dam, Grand Falls Dam, Milltown Dam, Woodland Dam), are indicated.
- A comprehensive legend defines symbols for structures, cities, the Canada-US border, provincial/state borders, lakes, rivers, and the St. Croix River Basin and adjacent Saint John River Basin boundaries.
- A compass rose for north orientation and dual scale bars in kilometers (0-40 km) and miles (0-20 miles) are included at the bottom.
