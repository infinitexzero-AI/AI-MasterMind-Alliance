# St. Croix River Basin Map
- This map illustrates the St. Croix River Basin, detailing the river system along the Canada-US border between the state of Maine and the province of New Brunswick.
- Key geographical features include the St. Croix River, various lakes (e.g., Grand Lake, Spednic Lake, Sysladobsis Lake), Tomah Stream, Passamaquoddy Bay, and the Bay of Fundy.
- Important locations such as cities (Danforth, Vanceboro, McAdam, Princeton, Saint Stephen, Calais, Saint Andrews) and structures (Forest City Dam, Vanceboro Dam, Grand Falls Dam, Milltown Dam, Woodland Dam) are marked.
- A legend provides symbols for structures, cities, international and provincial/state borders, lakes, rivers, and outlines for the St. Croix and Saint John River Basins.
- The map includes a directional compass, a scale bar in kilometers and miles, and lists Esri, USGS, and NOAA as sources.
