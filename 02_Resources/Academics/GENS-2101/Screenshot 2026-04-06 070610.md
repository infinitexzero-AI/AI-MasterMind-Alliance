# Forest Transition Model: How forests change over time

This slide presents a model depicting the four stages of forest cover change over time, aligned with a progression of 'Development'.

- **Stage 1 Pre-transition:** Characterized by high forest cover with very slow or no loss (less than 0.25%). The graph shows high forest cover.
- **Stage 2 Early transition:** Marked by an increasing deforestation rate, leading to a rapid decline in forest cover.
- **Stage 3 Late transition:** Forest cover is low, but the rate of deforestation is slowing, indicating a gradual reduction in forest cover.
- **Stage 4 Post-transition:** Forest cover remains low but is actively increasing through reforestation efforts. The graph shows forest cover increasing after reaching its lowest point.

Source: Our World in Data.
