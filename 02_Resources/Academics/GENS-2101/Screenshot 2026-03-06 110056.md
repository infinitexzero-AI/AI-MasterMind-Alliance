# Annual change in forest area, 1991 to 2025
-   **Description:** The visualization illustrates the net change in global forest area, defined as forest expansion (afforestation and natural expansion) minus deforestation, between 1991 and 2025.
-   **Visual Content:** Two choropleth world maps compare annual forest area change for the years 1991 and 2025. A color gradient legend ranges from deep red (-300,000 ha, significant deforestation) through orange/yellow (less change) to deep green (+300,000 ha, significant forest expansion).
-   **Key Observations:**
    -   **1991 Map:** Displays notable deforestation (red areas) in regions like South America (e.g., Brazil), Central Africa, and parts of Southeast Asia. Forest expansion (green areas) is visible in North America, Europe, and China.
    -   **2025 Map:** Shows a continuation of high deforestation rates in parts of South America and Central Africa. However, it also indicates increased forest expansion across Europe and parts of Asia (e.g., China, India) compared to the 1991 data.
-   **Data Source:** Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (2025). Data is based on average annual change over ten- or five-year periods.
-   **URL:** https://ourworldindata.org/deforestation
