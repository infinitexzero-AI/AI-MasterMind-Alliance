# Wetlands and Ecosystem Services
- Ecosystem services are benefits humans derive from natural ecosystems, crucial for human health, economic development, and overall quality of life (OMNRF, 2017).
- These services represent 40.6% of total global ecosystem services (ES), contributing about US$47.4 trillion per year to global economies (Constanza et al., 2014; Davidson et al., 2019; Convention on Wetlands, 2021).
- Canadian wetlands are estimated to provide ecosystem services worth about $225 billion per year, equivalent to around 10 percent of GDP (Institute for Sustainable finance, 2024).
- Attempts at the valuation of Ontario Wetlands indicate:
    - Suspended sediment and phosphorus filtration and removal capacity by Southern Ontario wetlands is $4.2B/yr, compared to a cost of $2.9B/year for artificial wetland construction to replace lost wetlands (Aziz and Van Cappellen, 2021).
    - The filtration ability of wetlands produces $40 billion in economic benefits annually (Troy & Bagstad, 2009).
    - Greenbelt wetlands contribute almost $1.3 billion in ecological services (Wilson, 2008).
- An accompanying infographic titled "Cultural and Spiritual Significance (MNRF, 2015)" with the University of Waterloo logo, visually lists key ecosystem services provided by wetlands, including: Water Quality Improvement, Wildlife Habitat, Fish Habitat, Flood Mitigation, Erosion Reduction, Climate Change Mitigation, Groundwater Recharge and Discharge, Recreation and Tourism, and Source of Food and Medicines.
