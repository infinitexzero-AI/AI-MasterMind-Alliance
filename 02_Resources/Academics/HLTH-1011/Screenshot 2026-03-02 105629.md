# Wetlands and Ecosystem Services
- Ecosystem services are defined as the benefits humans derive from natural ecosystems, crucial for human health, economic development, and overall quality of life.
- Globally, these services contribute an estimated US$47.4 trillion per year to economies. Canadian wetlands alone provide approximately $225 billion annually, equating to about 10% of GDP.
- Valuation attempts for Ontario wetlands indicate substantial economic benefits, including $4.2 billion/year for sediment and phosphorus filtration (versus $2.9 billion/year for artificial wetland construction), $40 billion annually from overall filtration, and an estimated $1.3 billion in ecological services from Greenbelt wetlands.
- A visual diagram illustrates ten key ecosystem services provided by wetlands: Water Quality Improvement, Source of Food and Medicines, Recreation and Tourism, Groundwater Recharge and Discharge, Climate Change Mitigation, Cultural and Spiritual Significance, Erosion Reduction, Flood Mitigation, Fish Habitat, and Wildlife Habitat.
