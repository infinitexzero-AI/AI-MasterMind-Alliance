# Ethical Space and Two-Eyed Seeing

The slide explores how to work together by examining different worldviews. It begins by stating that worldviews are not binary, but fluid with overlaps and chasms (Hart, 2010). The central question is, "How are we going to work together?"

It contrasts an **Indigenous worldview** described as "Wholistic" with a **Western approach**:

**Indigenous worldview (Wholistic):**
- Spirituality and ceremony are centred
- Relational accountability
- Relational/relationships
- Connection to the land and animals

**Western approach:**
- Positivist
- Based in scientific thought first and foremost
- Focuses on "only observable phenomena" (Brant-Castellano, 2004, 103)
- A reductionist approach to analysis (Brant-Castellano, 2004, 104)

"Ethical Space" and "Two-eyed seeing" are presented as bridging concepts between these approaches. A colourful, circular, woven/beaded art piece is displayed on the right side of the slide.
