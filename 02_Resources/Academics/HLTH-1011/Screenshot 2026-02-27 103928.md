# UN Water Integrated Monitoring Initiative for SDG 6

This slide presents the United Nations' Integrated Monitoring Initiative for Sustainable Development Goal 6 (SDG 6): Clean Water and Sanitation. It comprises a circular diagram illustrating the goal's components and a table detailing indicators and their custodians.

## SDG 6 Monitoring Framework

The central diagram is an infographic wheel for "6 Clean Water and Sanitation," broken down into key targets and indicators, each with associated monitoring entities:

*   **6.1.1 Drinking Water:** UN-Water GLAAS
*   **6.2.1a Sanitation & 6.2.1b Hygiene:** WHO/UNICEF JMP
*   **6.3.1 Wastewater & 6.3.2 Water Quality:** UNSD/UNEP/OECD GEMS/Water
*   **6.4.1 Water-Use Efficiency & 6.4.2 Water Stress:** FAO AQUASTAT
*   **6.5.1 Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM) & 6.5.2 Transboundary Cooperation:** Reporting under UN Water Convention (highlighted in pink/purple)
*   **6.6.1 Ecosystems:** Freshwater Ecosystems Explorer
*   **6.a.1 International Cooperation:** UN-Water GLAAS
*   **6.b.1 Participation:** UN-Water GLAAS

## Indicators and Custodians

A table lists each indicator, its full description, and the responsible international custodian organizations:

*   **6.1.1 Proportion of population using safely managed drinking water services:** WHO, UNICEF
*   **6.2.1 Proportion of population using (a) safely managed sanitation services and (b) a hand-washing facility with soap and water:** WHO, UNICEF
*   **6.3.1 Proportion of domestic and industrial wastewater flows safely treated:** WHO, UN-Habitat, UNSD
*   **6.3.2 Proportion of bodies of water with good ambient water quality:** UNEP
*   **6.4.1 Change in water-use efficiency over time:** FAO
*   **6.4.2 Level of water stress: freshwater withdrawal as a proportion of available freshwater resources:** FAO
*   **6.5.1 Degree of integrated water resources management:** UNEP
*   **6.5.2 Proportion of transboundary basin area with an operational arrangement for water cooperation:** UNECE, UNESCO
*   **6.6.1 Change in the extent of water-related ecosystems over time:** UNEP, Ramsar
*   **6.a.1 Amount of water- and sanitation-related official development assistance that is part of a government-coordinated spending plan:** WHO, OECD
*   **6.b.1 Proportion of local administrative units with established and operational policies and procedures for participation of local communities in water and sanitation management:** WHO, OECD
