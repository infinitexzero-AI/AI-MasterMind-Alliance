# Threats to Wetlands Sustainability
- 70% of wetlands in settled regions of Canada have either been degraded or destroyed (OBC, 2021).
- 20% of Canada's and 54% of Ontario's population resides in the 'golden horseshoe'.
- Ontario Biodiversity Council (2021) reports that between 2011 and 2015, a total of 7,303 hectares of wetlands were lost, surpassing the 6,152 hectares lost in Ontario from 2000 to 2011.
- Wetlands in southwestern Ontario, parts of eastern Ontario, Niagara and the Toronto area have experienced the greatest wetland loss, with over 85 percent of original pre-settlement wetlands converted to other uses (Ducks Unlimited Canada, 2010).
- The slide features two maps of southern Ontario illustrating wetland loss (in hectares) for the periods 2000-2011 and 2011-2015, indicating regional variations in the severity of loss (Source: Ontario Biodiversity Council, 2021).
