# Confounding
- Finding an association for the wrong reason.
- The variable is associated with both the risk factor and the disease being studied, but need not be a risk factor for the disease under study. The confounding variable can either inflate or deflate the true relative risk.
- Confounding in epidemiological studies is both a common and important phenomenon. Many, many variables may be confounders in any given study. Some of their effects may be small, others may be large.
- Given the large number of such factors, it is never possible to account for all potential confounders.
