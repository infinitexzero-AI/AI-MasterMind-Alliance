# The Forest Carbon Cycle
- **Atmospheric Exchange**:
  - Growing forests remove CO2 from the atmosphere through photosynthesis.
  - Plant respiration and soil microbial processes release CO2.
  - Carbon in forests and soils can be lost through disturbances like fires, pests, or storms, releasing CO2.
  - Combustion of lower value residues produces energy and releases CO2 to the atmosphere.
- **Forest & Soil Carbon Flow**:
  - Roots and decay of litter and deadwood transfer carbon to the soil.
  - Trees are harvested during forest management.
- **Timber & Wood Products Cycle**:
  - Harvested timber is processed into wood products.
  - Carbon is retained in wood products, which can be recycled or eventually disposed of via energy recovery.
  - Lower value residues, such as wood processing byproducts, are generated.
