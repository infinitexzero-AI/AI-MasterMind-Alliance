# Table 6.1 Understanding water scarcity: categorization of factors behind water scarcity according to the Falkenmark framework

This table outlines four modes of water scarcity, detailing their type, manifestation, and additional features:

- **Mode A: Aridity (Green water)**
    - **Manifestation:** Short growing season, determined by annual rainfall and potential evaporation.
    - **Additional features:** Sensitivity linked to crop choice.
- **Mode B: Drought (Green and blue water)**
    - **Manifestation:** Recurrent interannual meteorological droughts.
    - **Additional features:** Linked to the El Niño phenomenon.
- **Mode C: Land degradation (Green water)**
    - **Manifestation:** High vulnerability resulting in extensive land degradation.
    - **Additional features:** May lead to man-made drought (i.e., soil moisture deficit) without experiencing Type B drought.
- **Mode D: Water crowding (Blue water)**
    - **Manifestation:** Very limited blue water surplus, leading to blue water scarcity, which is exacerbated by population growth.
    - **Additional features:** Blue water scarcity; in the Savanna zone, this is specifically where there is less than 100 mm/year of runoff surplus.
