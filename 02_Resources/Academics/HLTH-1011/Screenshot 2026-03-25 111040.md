# Biofuel production by region
- The chart illustrates the global biofuel production by region from 1990 to 2024, measured in terawatt-hours (TWh) per year, including both bioethanol and biodiesel.
- Total biofuel production has shown a steep increase, growing from approximately 50 TWh in 1990 to over 1,300 TWh by 2024.
- The most significant period of growth occurred between 2000 and 2010.
- North America consistently represents the largest share of global biofuel production throughout the depicted period.
- South and Central America, and Europe are also substantial contributors, with their production increasing steadily.
- Other regions, including Asia Pacific, Africa, CIS (Commonwealth of Independent States), and the Middle East, contribute smaller but growing amounts to the total.
- The data source is the Energy Institute - Statistical Review of World Energy (2025).
