# Stages of Medical Research and Clinical Trials
- **Exploratory Research:** Researchers identify promising medicines or other health interventions for further studies.
- **Pre-clinical Research:** Researchers conduct laboratory and animal studies before testing in humans.
- **Clinical Trials:
  - **Phase I:** Determines if the medicine/intervention is safe in healthy volunteers, identifies a safe dose, and checks for side effects.
  - **Phase II:** Evaluates how well the medicine/intervention works in people with the condition, assesses side effects, and finds the safest and most effective dose.
  - **Phase III:** Further assesses how well the medicine/intervention works in people with the condition and monitors side effects in a larger population.
  - **Phase IV:** Post-market trials to study the medicine/intervention after it is approved and on the market.
- CIHR funds all of these stages of research.
