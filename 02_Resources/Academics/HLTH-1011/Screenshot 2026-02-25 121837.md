# Stages of Medicine and Intervention Research

This infographic outlines the progression of research and development for medicines and health interventions, from initial discovery to post-market surveillance.

*   **Exploratory Research:** Researchers identify promising medicines or other health interventions for further studies.
*   **Pre-clinical Research:** Researchers conduct laboratory and animal studies before testing in humans.
*   **Clinical Trials:**
    *   **Phase I:**
        *   Focus: Safety in healthy volunteers.
        *   Key Questions: Is it safe? What is a safe dose? Are there any side effects?
    *   **Phase II:**
        *   Focus: Efficacy and safety in people with the condition under study.
        *   Key Questions: How well does it work? What are the side effects? What is the safest and most effective dose?
    *   **Phase III:**
        *   Focus: Efficacy and side effects in a larger population with the condition.
        *   Key Questions: How well does it work? What are the side effects?
    *   **Phase IV:**
        *   Focus: Post-market studies after approval.
        *   Purpose: To continue studying the medicine or intervention after it is on the market.

**Funding:** CIHR funds all of these stages of research.
