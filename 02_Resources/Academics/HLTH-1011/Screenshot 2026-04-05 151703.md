# World population development
- An area chart illustrating global population trends from 1750 to a projection in 2050, measured in billions.
- The chart differentiates between two categories:
    - **Developing countries (teal)**: Shows an exponential increase in population, especially from the mid-20th century, projected to reach over 8 billion by 2050.
    - **Industrialized countries (orange)**: Shows a more gradual and slower increase, projected to be slightly over 1 billion by 2050, forming a smaller proportion of the total.
- The overall trend indicates rapid, exponential growth of the world population, predominantly driven by developing countries.
- Source: GRID UNEP Arendal.
