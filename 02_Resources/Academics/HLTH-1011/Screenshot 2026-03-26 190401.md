# Factors Influencing Agricultural Production

This diagram illustrates a hierarchical model showing how various factors influence actual biomass production, moving from theoretical potential to the farmer's reality.

-   **Potential Biomass Production (Theory)** is determined by fundamental biophysical conditions, including radiation, temperature, crop physiology, satisfaction of crop requirements, and soil characteristics (nutrients, structure, depth).
-   **What Can Be Produced (On-station)** is further constrained by environmental preconditions (e.g., droughts, floods), inherent soil fertility limitations, soil and water management practices, and the availability of improved farming systems (e.g., tree/crop mixes).
-   **What Will Be Produced (Farmers' Opportunities)** is shaped by socio-economic incentives and disincentives, encompassing factors such as land tenure, marketing access, infrastructure availability, gender roles, and individual farmer perceptions.
-   **What Is Produced (Farmers' Reality)** represents the actual output, which is a consequence of the practiced farming systems and the extent of land degradation.
