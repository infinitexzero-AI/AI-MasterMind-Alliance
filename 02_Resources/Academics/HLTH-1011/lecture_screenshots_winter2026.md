## Topic: Systematic Reviews and Health Research Methods

### Key Characteristics of a Systematic Review
- clearly stated objectives;
- pre-defined eligibility criteria;
- explicit, reproducible methodology;
- systematic search of the literature;
- assessment of validity of included studies;
- systematic synthesis and presentation of findings.

### Systematic Review Definition
- A systematic review uses robust methods to reduce bias in the gathering, summarizing, presenting, interpreting, and reporting of the research evidence (Cochrane Reviews, 2023).
- Uses a pre-determined and comprehensive searching and screening method to identify relevant articles, in order to minimize bias (Introduction to Health Research Methods: A Practical Guide, Chapter 15).

### Recall Bias
- **Definition:** Sometimes the subjects' ability to recall and report past experiences may be affected by their preconceived ideas about a possible health hazard.
- **Example A:** Those who believe that exposure to a certain substance is the cause of their ill health are likely to be able to document many such occurrences.
- **Example B:** Subjects who do not blame the exposure for their health status, may have experienced exactly the same illnesses, but since they did not associate their illness with the exposure, they may not have made mental notes of when those illnesses occurred.
