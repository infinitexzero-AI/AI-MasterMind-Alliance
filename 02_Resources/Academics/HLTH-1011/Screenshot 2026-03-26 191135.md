# Socio-Economic
This slide outlines key socio-economic factors and questions related to food systems, agriculture, and development.
-   **Market Forces:** Investigates how local, national, regional, and global market forces shape supply chains, manage surplus production, influence farmer crop choices, and interact to impact farming practices.
-   **Supply/Demand Pressures:** Explores urban and global sources of supply/demand, differentiating between cash crops, food crops, and crops for global tastes or industrial use (mining). It also questions who determines farmer supports (e.g., seed varietals, fertilizer, water infrastructure) and how demands for food/cash crops at various scales influence farming practices and government/private sector investment decisions.
-   **Job Creation:** Examines how job pressures affect land use practices.
-   **Revenue Generation, Economic Development, Food Security, Political Stability:** Addresses how governments set priorities and the role of farmers in decision-making processes related to these critical areas.
