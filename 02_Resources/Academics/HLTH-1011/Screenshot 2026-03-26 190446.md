# Grain Yield Response to Rainfall and Limiting Factors
-   The graph illustrates grain yield (kg ha⁻¹) against rainfall (mm yr⁻¹).
-   The solid line represents "Simulated water limited production," showing the potential yield limited only by water. "Yields without nutrient deficit" (triangles) generally follow this line.
-   Farmers' actual yields from Ouagadougou (squares) and Ouaigouya (diamonds) are considerably lower than the water-limited potential.
-   A dotted line indicates the "upper threshold of farmers' yields," which are limited by both water and nutrients, showing a plateau at a much lower yield level than the water-limited potential.
-   The data suggests that nutrient deficiencies, alongside water availability, are significant constraints on farmers' grain yields in these regions.
