## WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN FOOD SECURITY AND FOOD SOVEREIGNTY?
Food security and food sovereignty are distinct concepts in the realm of food systems, each addressing different aspects of food access and control.

### FOOD SECURITY
- Refers to a condition where all individuals have consistent access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food to meet their dietary needs for an active and healthy life.
- Focuses on the availability, accessibility, and utilisation of food, often emphasising large-scale production and market mechanisms to ensure food is available to populations, particularly during crises.
- Efforts to achieve food security typically involve increasing food production and stabilising food prices, often relying on industrial agricultural practices.

### FOOD SOVEREIGNTY
- Emphasises the right of communities to define their own food systems and policies. It advocates for local control over food production and distribution, prioritising sustainable practices and cultural relevance.
- This concept challenges the corporate-driven models of food security by promoting small-scale farming, environmental sustainability, and social justice.
- Food sovereignty is rooted in the belief that communities should have the power to shape their agricultural practices based on their unique ecological and cultural contexts, thus addressing the root causes of food insecurity.
