## What is the Difference Between Food Security and Food Sovereignty?
Food security and food sovereignty are distinct concepts in the realm of food systems, each addressing different aspects of food access and control.

### Food Security
- **Definition**: A condition where all individuals have consistent access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food to meet their dietary needs for an active and healthy life.
- **Focus**: Availability, accessibility, and utilisation of food, often emphasizing large-scale production and market mechanisms to ensure food is available to populations, particularly during crises.
- **Efforts**: Typically involve increasing food production and stabilising food prices, often relying on industrial agricultural practices.

### Food Sovereignty
- **Definition**: Emphasises the right of communities to define their own food systems and policies. It advocates for local control over food production and distribution, prioritising sustainable practices and cultural relevance.
- **Challenge**: This concept challenges the corporate-driven models of food security by promoting small-scale farming, environmental sustainability, and social justice.
- **Rooted In**: The belief that communities should have the power to shape their agricultural practices based on their unique ecological and cultural contexts, thus addressing the root causes of food insecurity.
