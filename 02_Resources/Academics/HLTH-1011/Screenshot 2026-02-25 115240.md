# Recall Bias
- Sometimes the subjects' ability to recall and report past experiences may be affected by their preconceived ideas about a possible health hazard.
- Those who believe that exposure to a certain substance is the cause of their ill health are likely to be able to document many such occurrences.
- Subjects who do not blame the exposure for their health status, may have experienced exactly the same illnesses, but since they did not associate their illness with the exposure, they may not have made mental notes of when those illnesses occurred.
