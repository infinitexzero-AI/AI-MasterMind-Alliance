### Scenario B: Adding a Specialized Undergraduate Certificate (12-18 Credits)
Certificates are a fantastic way to cluster extra coursework around a specific theme without the heavier burden of a full minor. They appear impressively on your degree.

1.  **Certificate in Biopsychology (18 Credits)**
    *   *Why*: Directly references the 2020/2021 calendar pathway and aligns perfectly with your biology major and rural mental health research interests by uniting the physiological basis for behavior.
    *   *Requirements*: 
        *   **6 credits** from `BIOL 2811` or `PSYC 2101`.
        *   **12 credits** from an approved list (requiring at least 3 credits each from Biology and Psychology).
        *   *Approved Courses*: `BIOL 3211`, `BIOL 3401`, `BIOL 4311`, `PSYC 3101`, `PSYC 3211`, `PSYC 4101`. (Note: Other courses with a significant biopsychology component may be approved by Department Heads).
2.  **Certificate in Foundations of Health (18 Credits)**
    *   *Why*: Fits seamlessly with your major and your interest in rural/telemental health. 
    *   *Requirements*: Needs BIOL 3211, BIOL 3221 or 3751, and two PSYC courses (2101 & 2611), plus some electives. Since you are a Biology major, you likely already have prerequisites and some overlapping course credits.
3.  **Certificate in Community Engaged Learning (18 Credits)**
    *   *Why*: Direct application to your goals in community integration and collaborative policy. It involves core theoretical courses and a 3-credit practicum where you work directly with a community partner.
4.  **Certificate in Mi'kmaw or Indigenous Studies**
