# Wetlands
-   **Definition (Ramsar):** Areas of marsh, fen, peatland, or water, whether natural or artificial, permanent or temporary, with water that is static or flowing, fresh, brackish or salt. It includes marine waters where depth at low tide does not exceed six metres, and riparian/coastal zones adjacent to wetlands, and islands or bodies of marine water deeper than six metres within the wetlands.
-   **Global Coverage:** Estimated to cover about 1,800 million hectares of Earth's surface.
-   **Broad Categories:** 11 broad types including Seagrass, Kelp Forests, Coral Reefs, Estuarine Waters, Salt Marshes, Mangroves, Tidal Flats, Lakes, Rivers and Streams, Inland Marshes and Swamps, and Peatlands (Mires).
-   **Ramsar Wetland Type Classification (Table 1):** Wetlands are classified into three main categories:
    -   **Marine/Coastal Wetlands:** Examples include permanent shallow marine waters, coral reefs, estuarine waters, intertidal marshes, and coastal brackish/saline lagoons.
    -   **Inland Wetlands:** Examples include permanent inland deltas, rivers/streams, permanent and seasonal freshwater/saline lakes, various types of marshes/pools, non-forested peatlands, alpine/tundra wetlands, and forested wetlands.
    -   **Human-made Wetlands:** Examples include aquaculture, ponds, irrigated land, wastewater treatment areas, canals, and water storage areas.
