# The Grand River Watershed & The Grand River Conservation Authority

- The Grand River Watershed is Southern Ontario's largest watershed, extending roughly 6800 km² from Dundalk to Lake Erie.
- The combined value of Grand River Wetlands is estimated at $1.4 billion.
- The watershed has lost approximately 65% (two-thirds) of its wetland area.
- The Grand River Conservation Authority is responsible for managing water and natural resources for 38 municipalities and nearly one million residents.
- A map visually depicts the Grand River Watershed, showing its geographical outline, major rivers (Grand River, Nith River, Speed River, Conestogo River, Eramosa River), lakes (Luther Lake, Conestogo Lake, Belwood Lake, Guelph Lake), and various towns and municipalities within its boundaries.
